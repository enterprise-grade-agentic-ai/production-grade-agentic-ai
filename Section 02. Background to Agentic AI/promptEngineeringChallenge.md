# Challenge: Prompt Engineering

> **Cost note:** This challenge only requires access to an LLM chat interface — the free tiers of ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude are more than enough. No API keys or paid plans needed.

This challenge is about trying out the prompt examples from the previous video, plus a few other prompting techniques. You can follow the steps below directly, or use them as a reference while exploring in your own way.

---

## Step 1: Pick Your LLM

Choose whichever LLM you prefer — ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, or any other. If you're taking this course, you've likely already got access to one.

---

## Step 2: Test the Prompt Examples from the Video

Try both the suboptimal and effective versions of each prompt and compare the results.

- **Prompt 1:**
  - *Suboptimal:* Write an email to my team
  - *Effective:* Act as a project manager. Write a professional and concise email to my software development team. Inform them that the project deadline for the mobile app has been extended by two weeks due to unexpected technical challenges.

- **Prompt 2:**
  - *Suboptimal:* Make a picture of a dog
  - *Effective:* You are an expert graphic designer. Generate a photorealistic image of a golden retriever puppy. The puppy should be sitting in a sunny, green meadow, looking directly at the camera with a playful expression.

---

## Step 3: Explore Prompting Techniques

The [Prompting Guide](https://www.promptingguide.ai/) is an excellent reference. Focus on these three techniques and try each out in your chosen LLM:

- [Zero-Shot Prompting](https://www.promptingguide.ai/techniques/zeroshot)
- [Few-Shot Prompting](https://www.promptingguide.ai/techniques/fewshot)
- [Chain of Thought Prompting](https://www.promptingguide.ai/techniques/cot)

This step will likely take most of your time — give it the attention it deserves before heading back to the course.
